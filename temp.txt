## Conclusions
**Results**: The Diode Tester successfully delivers a stable, selectable constant current regardless of the supply voltage used ($12\text{V}$ to $30\text{V}$). The LM358 unity-gain differential configuration isolates the high-side floating measurement perfectly, providing a linear, ground-referenced voltage to the 3-wire digital panel meter. The current regulation feedback loop ($Q_2/Q_3$) handles the transitions between low-impedance forward diodes and high-voltage reverse Zener breakdowns without oscillations or current spikes.

**Suggestions**:
- Replace the input protection diode $D_3$ with a P-channel MOSFET if lower input voltage overhead is desired, though with $12\text{V}-30\text{V}$ inputs, the $0.7\text{V}$ drop of the 1N4148 is negligible.
- Add a small 100nF ceramic capacitor in parallel with the TEST terminals to suppress high-frequency noise when testing high-voltage Zeners near their knee voltage region.

**Evolutions**: 
- Design an upgraded version integrating a secondary microcontroller-driven stage to automatically sweep the current through 8 logarithmic steps, allowing the automated plotting of the $I/V$ characteristic curve on a small OLED display or via serial output.
